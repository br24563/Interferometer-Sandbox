Physical Model

For equal beam intensities with coherence factor γ:

I/(IA + IB) = ½[1 + γ cos(φ)]

Where:

  • φ = 2π(OPD)/λ + φ₀ — phase difference in radians
  • OPD — uses the selected instrument’s signed path-difference definition
  • γ ∈ [0,1] — degree of coherence (temporal coherence)
  • IA + IB — sum of the individual returning beam intensities (assumed equal)

Measurement Tips

  • Use numeric inputs for precise picometer-scale adjustments; use sliders for rapid exploration.
  • Use the unit selector to enter arm lengths in nm, µm, or mm; all arm displays update together.
  • Adjust coherence to see how fringe contrast degrades with realistic light sources.
  • In Michelson mode, moving mirror B by λ/4 changes the round-trip phase by π, taking a bright fringe to a dark fringe. A λ/2 move completes one 2π fringe cycle.
  • At constructive interference (phase ≈ 0, 2π, …), intensity is maximum; at destructive interference (phase ≈ π, 3π, …), it is zero.
  • The Visibility is the ratio of (Imax − Imin) / (Imax + Imin), capped by coherence.
