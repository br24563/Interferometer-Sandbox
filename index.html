Physical Model

Lab Reference

Michelson

  • Light travels to each mirror and returns — OPD = 2(LB − LA).
  • λ/2 mirror scan = one complete fringe cycle (Δφ = 2π).
  • Fringe visibility directly equals γ (coherence degree).

Mach–Zehnder

  • Light traverses each arm once — OPD = n·LB − LA.
  • Insert a sample in arm B: Δn × L gives the OPD shift.
  • Commonly used in flow visualization and integrated optics.

Fabry–Pérot

  • Multiple reflections produce narrow Airy-function resonances.
  • Finesse ℱ = π√R / (1 − R); resolving power = m × ℱ.
  • Free Spectral Range FSR = λ² / (2nL).

Sagnac

  • Counter-propagating beams sense rotation via OPD = 4AΩ/c.
  • Encircled area A and rotation rate Ω determine sensitivity.
  • Fibre-optic gyroscopes use many wound loops to amplify A.
